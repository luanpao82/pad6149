## What Is This Assignment?

You've identified a role-model organization (Week 2) and conducted an informational interview with one of its leaders. Now write it up: what did you learn, and how will it shape the nonprofit you're building?

This is a **service learning assignment** — the value flows both ways. You gain practitioner insight; the leader you interviewed gains exposure to the next generation of nonprofit professionals. Many of these connections last well beyond the semester.

---

## What Do I Submit?

A **4–5 page, double-spaced** report covering:

### 1. Organization Profile (approximately 1 page)
- Organization name, mission, founding year
- Size (approximate budget, staff count)
- Key programs and services
- Primary funding sources
- Geographic scope and populations served

### 2. The Interview Process (approximately half a page)
- How you identified and contacted this person
- Format (phone, **Zoom**, in-person) and duration
- What the outreach process itself taught you about professional networking in the sector

If your interview is virtual, use **Zoom's** built-in recording feature (free tier allows 40-minute meetings). If your interviewee agrees to being recorded, **Otter.ai** (otter.ai) can transcribe the conversation in real time — this lets you stay present in the conversation instead of scrambling to take notes. Always ask permission before recording.

### 3. Key Insights from the Interview (approximately 1.5–2 pages)
- Summarize the most substantive questions and responses
- Don't transcribe the entire conversation — focus on the insights that were most relevant, surprising, or useful
- Connect their answers to concepts from the course readings where applicable
- If the interviewee mentions a statistic, policy, or trend you want to include, verify it using **Perplexity.ai** — cited sources strengthen your report

After the interview, upload your transcript or notes to **Google NotebookLM** and ask it to identify recurring themes and surprising insights across the conversation. This can help you see patterns you might have missed in the moment. Then use **ChatGPT or Claude** to help organize your raw notes into thematic sections — but write the actual report in your own voice.

### 4. Takeaways for Your Nonprofit (approximately 1 page)
- 3–5 specific lessons you will apply to your own organization's design
- Be concrete: not "I learned that funding is important" but rather a specific, actionable insight tied to your organizational design

> [!example] Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Interview Takeaway
> **Takeaway #3: Language access is infrastructure, not a program.**
>
> Ms. Kim emphasized that AARC doesn't treat translation and interpretation as a standalone program — it's embedded in everything they do. "If a family can't understand what's happening, nothing else we offer matters," she said. This reframed how I'm thinking about Asian Bridge Orlando's design. Rather than listing "language services" as one program among many, I'll build multilingual capacity into the organizational infrastructure itself — bilingual staff as a hiring priority, translated materials as a default, and interpretation available at every point of contact.

### 5. Reflection (approximately half a page)
- How did this conversation change or refine your thinking about your nonprofit concept?
- Did anything surprise you? Challenge your assumptions?
- What would you ask if you had another hour?

### 6. Contact Information
- Interviewee name, title, organization, email, phone
