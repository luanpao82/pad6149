## What Is This Assignment?

This is the integration assignment. Everything you've built over the past five weeks — governance, staffing, brand identity, budget, fundraising — now comes together into a **cohesive organizational strategy**.

In PAD 6335, you developed a Strategic Plan for an existing nonprofit. You analyzed their environment, assessed strengths and weaknesses, defined strategic goals, and proposed action plans. Now you're doing the same thing — but for the organization you've been building from scratch.

The difference: when you wrote a strategic plan for someone else's organization, you were working with historical data, existing programs, and established stakeholders. Here, you're projecting forward from zero. That requires a different kind of strategic thinking — less about where the organization has been, and more about where it needs to go and what it takes to get there.

## What Do I Submit?

### 1. Executive Summary (1 page)

A concise overview of the entire strategic plan — mission, vision, key strategic goals, and Year 1 priorities. This should stand alone: a board member or funder who reads only this page should understand your organization's direction.

> [!example] Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Executive Summary
> Asian Bridge Orlando was founded to address a critical gap in Central Florida's social service landscape: the lack of culturally and linguistically responsive services for Asian American and immigrant families. Our mission — connecting families to the services, language access, and community they need to thrive — guides every aspect of our organizational strategy.
>
> In Year 1, we will focus on three strategic priorities: (1) establishing core community navigation services in partnership with existing agencies, (2) building a sustainable funding base through diversified revenue streams, and (3) developing organizational capacity through staff recruitment, volunteer engagement, and board development. Our $200,000 operating budget supports 5 staff positions and a volunteer corps of 20–25 community members.
>
> This plan was developed through analysis of the local service landscape, stakeholder input from our mentor interview, and application of strategic planning frameworks from the MNM curriculum. It is designed to position Asian Bridge Orlando for growth while maintaining the community trust essential to our mission.

### 2. Organizational Overview (1 page)

Summarize the key elements from your previous assignments:
- Mission and vision (from Week 6)
- Legal structure and 501(c)(3) status (from Week 1)
- Board composition and governance structure (from Week 4)
- Staffing and volunteer plan (from Week 5)
- Brand identity and target audiences (from Week 6)

This section should read as a coherent narrative — not a list of things you did in previous weeks. Synthesize.

### 3. Environmental Analysis (2 pages)

#### SWOT Analysis

Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis that integrates your fundraising SWOT (Week 8) with broader organizational and environmental factors.

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
| **External** | **Opportunities** | **Threats** |

Use **Perplexity.ai** to research current trends and data relevant to your SWOT: *"What are the current trends in funding for immigrant-serving nonprofits in Florida? What policy changes might affect these organizations in 2026–2027?"*

#### Needs Assessment Summary

Summarize the community need your organization addresses. Draw on:
- Data from **Census Reporter** and **Social Explorer** (UCF Library) that you gathered in Week 1
- Insights from your mentor interview (Week 4)
- Any relevant academic literature from **Google Scholar**

#### Competitive Landscape

Who else is working in your space? This isn't about competition in the business sense — it's about understanding where your organization fits in the ecosystem.
- What organizations serve similar populations?
- What gaps remain that your organization addresses?
- How will you collaborate rather than duplicate?

Use **Candid / GuideStar** (candid.org) to research comparable organizations' programs, budgets, and service areas.

> [!example] Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Competitive Landscape
> Central Florida has several organizations serving immigrant populations, including the Asian American Resource Center (AARC), Hispanic Federation of Central Florida, and Catholic Charities' refugee services. However, our analysis reveals a specific gap: no organization currently provides comprehensive, culturally responsive navigation services tailored to the diversity *within* the Asian American community — addressing the distinct needs of East Asian, South Asian, and Southeast Asian families through linguistically appropriate services.
>
> Asian Bridge Orlando does not seek to replace existing organizations. Our strategy is to complement them — partnering with AARC for legal clinic referrals, with Catholic Charities for refugee-specific services, and with UCF for research and volunteer recruitment. Our distinctive contribution is the bridge function: connecting families to the right service at the right time, in their language, with cultural understanding.

### 4. Strategic Goals and Objectives (2–3 pages)

Define **3–5 strategic goals** for Years 1–3. For each goal, include:

- **Goal statement** — broad, directional
- **Objectives** — specific, measurable outcomes (use SMART criteria)
- **Key activities** — what you'll actually do
- **Timeline** — when it happens
- **Resources needed** �� budget, staff, partnerships
- **How you'll measure success** — specific metrics

> [!tip] AI Tip: Strategic Goals Framework
> Use **ChatGPT or Claude** to stress-test your goals:
> ```
> Review these 3 strategic goals for a startup nonprofit with a
> $200,000 Year 1 budget and 5 staff members. Are they realistic?
> Are the objectives measurable? What's missing? What would you
> prioritize if resources are tighter than expected?
>
> Goal 1: [paste your goal]
> Goal 2: [paste your goal]
> Goal 3: [paste your goal]
> ```

> [!example] Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Strategic Goal 1
> **Goal 1: Establish core community navigation services by end of Year 1.**
>
> | Objective | Key Activities | Timeline | Metrics |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | Serve 150 families through one-on-one navigation | Hire 2 bilingual Community Navigators; develop intake process; establish referral partnerships with 10 agencies | Q1–Q2 for hiring; Q2–Q4 for service delivery | # families served, # referrals made, client satisfaction surveys |
> | Launch 6 community workshops | Design curriculum for healthcare enrollment, school registration, and civic participation workshops; partner with libraries and community centers for space | Q2–Q4 | # workshops held, # attendees, pre/post knowledge assessments |
> | Build a multilingual resource library | Translate key documents into Mandarin, Vietnamese, Burmese/Karen, and Nepali; create digital and print versions | Q1–Q3 | # languages covered, # documents translated, # downloads/distributions |
>
> **Resources:** 2 FTE Community Navigators ($80,000), Program Director oversight, $18,000 program budget, volunteer translators
>
> *This goal directly addresses our mission and represents the core service that justifies our existence. If we achieve nothing else in Year 1, we must demonstrate that families are being reached and connected to services.*

### 5. Financial Strategy Summary (1 page)

Connect your budget (Week 7) and fundraising plan (Week 8) to your strategic goals:
- How does the budget allocation support each strategic goal?
- What is the fundraising timeline, and how does it align with program launch?
- What happens if revenue falls short of projections? What's your contingency?
- What are the financial benchmarks for Year 1 success?

Use **Google Sheets** to create a simple strategic budget alignment table showing which budget line items support which strategic goals.

### 6. Implementation Timeline (1 page)

Create a visual Year 1 implementation timeline that maps strategic goals, key milestones, and deadlines across all 12 months. Use **Google Sheets** or **Canva** to create this — a table or Gantt-style chart works well.

> [!example] Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Year 1 Milestones
> | Quarter | Governance & Operations | Programs | Fundraising |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | **Q1** (Jan–Mar) | Incorporate; file 501(c)(3); hire ED and Program Director; first board meeting | Develop intake process; begin partnership outreach | Submit 2 grant applications; launch board giving campaign |
> | **Q2** (Apr–Jun) | Hire Community Navigators; onboard volunteers; launch website | Begin serving families; first community workshop | Corporate sponsorship outreach; individual donor cultivation |
> | **Q3** (Jul–Sep) | Mid-year board review; first program evaluation | 100 families served; 4 workshops completed | Fall fundraising event; mid-year donor update |
> | **Q4** (Oct–Dec) | Annual board retreat; Year 2 planning begins | 150 families served; multilingual resource library launched | Year-end giving campaign; annual report published |

### 7. Evaluation Framework (half a page)

How will you know if the strategic plan is working? Define:
- Key performance indicators (KPIs) for each strategic goal
- How and when you'll collect data
- Who is responsible for monitoring progress
- When and how the plan will be reviewed and updated

This section sets up your **Program Evaluation Framework** assignment in Week 10.
