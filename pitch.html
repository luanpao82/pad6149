The tool stack

AI is required, not prohibited.

Practicing nonprofit leaders already use AI for grant writing, donor communications, and program evaluation. Prohibiting it leaves graduates unprepared. Requiring it — with a documented ethical framework — prepares them.

ChatGPT · Claude
Writing · Reasoning
Drafting, revision, and critique across all fourteen weeks — mission statements, policies, budget narratives, donor communications, pitch scripts.
NotebookLM
Reactivation
Upload prior PAD coursework — Governance (6237), HR (6417), Budgeting (6208), Fundraising (5146), Strategy (6335), Evaluation (6327), Advocacy (6142) — and adapt it for the new organization.
Canva
Visual Design
Logos, org charts, budget visualizations, strategy infographics, donor graphics, social content, pitch video title cards.
Gemini
Research
Current-events grounding and research depth when drafting strategic plans and policy briefs.
Web Builders

What students take with them

Six durable artifacts.

Not a transcript. Not a reflection paper. Real things they can point a hiring manager at.

01

A complete organizational blueprint

Mission, governance, staffing, budget, strategy, programs, evaluation, fundraising, advocacy — for an organization of their own design.

02

A live public website

Shareable, indexable, linkable from a résumé. Functions as a portfolio piece for employers and funders.

03

A final pitch video

A YouTube-hosted pitch presenting the organization to potential supporters — interview artifact or writing sample.

04

Documented AI fluency

Critical AI use across fourteen weeks of professional deliverables — a growing sector requirement.

05

A practitioner network connection

A completed mentor interview and relationship with a working nonprofit leader in their focus area.

06

Fresh NASPAA evidence

Rubric-aligned demonstration across all five competencies, on newly-produced work — not reflections on past courses.
