What Is This Assignment?

You've spent 11 weeks building a nonprofit on paper and on a website. This week, you bring it to life visually — and share it with the real world.

AI tools have made it possible for anyone to create professional-quality video and visual content without a production budget, a design team, or video editing skills. As a nonprofit leader, this is a skill you need: the ability to tell your organization's story visually, quickly, and compellingly.

This week you will:

  1. Create a 30–60 second promotional video for your nonprofit using AI tools
  2. Create 3–5 promotional images using AI image generation
  3. Share your content on LinkedIn with a post introducing your coursework
  4. Complete a final website audit before your pitch presentation

Part 1: Create Your Nonprofit's Promotional Video

Use AI video tools to create a 30–60 second video that introduces your organization. Think of it as a visual elevator pitch — the kind of video that would play on your homepage, at a fundraising event, or in a grant application.

What your video should include:

  • Organization name and logo
  • The problem you're solving (1–2 sentences)
  • What your organization does (1–2 sentences)
  • A call to action ("Learn more," "Get involved," "Support our work")

Recommended Tools (all free):

Option A: Canva Video (Recommended — easiest)

  1. Go to Canva (canva.com) → search "nonprofit video" or "promotional video"
  2. Select a template → customize with your org name, mission, colors, and images
  3. Use Canva's AI features: Magic Design for layout suggestions, text-to-image for custom visuals, and Magic Write for script suggestions
  4. Add background music from Canva's free library
  5. Export as MP4

Option B: Lumen5 (lumen5.com)

  1. Free tier available → paste your About Us text or mission statement
  2. Lumen5's AI automatically generates a video from your text — selects images, creates scenes, adds transitions
  3. Customize colors, fonts, and music
  4. Export as MP4 (free tier includes Lumen5 watermark)

Option C: InVideo AI (invideo.io)

  1. Free tier available → describe your nonprofit in a prompt
  2. InVideo's AI generates a complete video draft with stock footage, text overlays, and music
  3. Edit scenes, swap images, adjust pacing
  4. Export as MP4

AI Tip: Video Script

Before opening any video tool, draft your script with ChatGPT or Claude:

Write a 30-second video script for a nonprofit called [Your Org Name].
Mission: [your mission statement].
The video should open with the problem, introduce the organization
as the solution, and end with a call to action. Tone: professional,
warm, urgent but hopeful. Keep it under 80 words.

Then use this script as the foundation for your video.

Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Video Script

[Scene 1: Aerial shot of Orlando skyline]
"Central Florida's Asian American and immigrant population has grown 45% in a decade."

[Scene 2: Family walking into a community center]
"But culturally responsive services haven't kept pace. Language barriers, isolation, and system complexity keep families from the resources they need."

[Scene 3: Community Navigator meeting with a family]
"Asian Bridge Orlando connects families to services, language access, and community — in their language, with cultural understanding."

[Scene 4: Logo + website URL]
"Learn more at asianbridgeorlando.org"

AI Image Generation

Use Canva's Magic Media (free, limited generations per month) or Microsoft Designer (designer.microsoft.com, free with Microsoft account):

Create a warm, professional photograph of a diverse community
gathering at a nonprofit event. Asian American families, community
workers, and volunteers in a welcoming community center setting.
Bright, natural lighting. No text overlay.

Review AI-generated images critically — check for visual artifacts, cultural accuracy, and whether the image genuinely represents your community.

AI Image Ethics

AI-generated images of people can raise ethical concerns, particularly when representing specific communities. If you use AI to generate images of people:

  • Do not present them as real individuals
  • Ensure representations are respectful and culturally appropriate
  • Consider using AI for abstract, symbolic, or graphic design images instead of photorealistic people
  • On your website, label AI-generated images as such if they could be mistaken for real photographs

Part 3: LinkedIn Post

You need a LinkedIn account for this part. If you don't have one, create a free profile at linkedin.com this week.

Important

You are not adding this nonprofit to your LinkedIn profile or experience section. This is a class project, not a real organization. You are posting one post about your coursework — clearly labeled as such.

Post Your Content

Create a single LinkedIn post sharing your AI-generated video or image and introducing the nonprofit you built for this course.

Your post must include:

  1. A brief introduction to the organization you designed (3–5 sentences)
  2. Your video or one of your promotional images attached
  3. A link to your website
  4. Required disclosure — the following statement must appear at the end of your post:

⚠️ This is not a real nonprofit organization. It was designed as part of my capstone course (PAD 6149 — Nonprofit Administration) in the Master of Nonprofit Management program at the University of Central Florida. The promotional video/images were created using AI tools as a course assignment. #UCF #MNM #NonprofitLeadership

This disclosure is not optional. It ensures your professional network understands the context.

AI Tip: LinkedIn Post Draft

Use ChatGPT or Claude to draft your post:

Write a LinkedIn post (under 200 words) sharing a capstone course
project. I designed a nonprofit called [Your Org Name] that serves
[population] in [area]. I want to share what I built and the AI-
generated promotional content I created. Tone: proud of the work,
clearly a student project, professional. Do NOT make it sound like
a real organization launch.

Edit the output to match your voice and make sure the disclosure is included.

Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — LinkedIn Post

"This semester in my MNM capstone course at UCF, I designed a nonprofit from the ground up: Asian Bridge Orlando — a concept for an organization connecting Central Florida's Asian American and immigrant families to culturally responsive services and language access.

Over 12 weeks, I built the full organizational blueprint: governance structure, staffing plan, $200K operating budget, fundraising strategy, program evaluation framework, and a strategic plan. The website is live as a demonstration of the coursework.

I also used AI tools (Canva, ChatGPT, NotebookLM) to create this promotional video — a skill I'll carry into my career in the nonprofit sector.

Check out the site: [website URL]

⚠️ This is not a real nonprofit organization. It was designed as part of my capstone course (PAD 6149 — Nonprofit Administration) in the Master of Nonprofit Management program at the University of Central Florida. The promotional video/images were created using AI tools as a course assignment. #UCF #MNM #NonprofitLeadership"
