Course Philosophy

This course operates on a simple premise: the best way to learn nonprofit management is to do nonprofit management.

Rather than reading about organizations and reflecting on past assignments, you will spend the semester building a real nonprofit organization — developing its mission, board, staff plan, programs, budget, marketing identity, and advocacy strategy. Every module's readings and concepts connect directly to a deliverable that makes your organization more complete.

You leave this course not just with a degree, but with a fully developed organizational blueprint — and in some cases, the beginnings of an organization you will actually launch.

AI as Professional Competency

Nonprofit leaders today use AI tools to write grants, communicate with donors, analyze program data, and develop strategy. This course requires students to use AI tools — and to reflect critically on what AI can and cannot do in the service of a mission-driven organization. Each assignment page includes a "Useful AI & Online Tools" table with specific free tools recommended for that week.

Capstone Project

Each student designs and develops a complete nonprofit organization over 14 weeks, leveraging a variety of AI tools — from Google NotebookLM and ChatGPT to Canva AI and Perplexity — to adapt prior MNM coursework into a fully operational organizational blueprint. Every assignment produces a document submitted to Webcourses and a corresponding page on the organization's website, culminating in a pitch-ready nonprofit with governance, staffing, programs, financials, and a public web presence.

AI Tools Policy

Rules of Ethical AI Use

  • You must author, direct, critically evaluate, and take ownership of all AI-assisted work
  • Submitting raw AI output without your own analysis, judgment, and revision is academic dishonesty
  • AI-generated text that you adopt must be revised and integrated into your own voice
  • AI-generated images must be labeled as such when they could be mistaken for real photographs

Free tools only. No paid subscriptions are required. Each assignment page includes a tools table listing specific free tools for that week.

Course Policies

Late Work

Late work may be accepted at the professor's discretion with a 10% deduction per day late (except University holidays). Emergencies can be discussed with the professor.

Academic Honesty

All work must be your own scholarly and creative efforts, per UCF's Golden Rule. Plagiarism, copying, and all other types of cheating will not be tolerated. Self-plagiarism — submitting work that is the same or substantially similar to work for which you have already gained university credit — also applies.

Accessibility

Students with disabilities who need accommodations must contact the professor at the beginning of the semester and be registered with Student Accessibility Services (Ferrell Commons 7F, Room 185, phone 407-823-2371).

Writing & APA Style

Graduate-level writing proficiency is expected. All citations use APA 7th edition. Resources: UCF Library APA Guide.
