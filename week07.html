What Is This Assignment?

Your nonprofit has a mission, governance, and a team. Now the question every funder will ask: how much does it cost, and where does the money come from?

You submit your budget and narrative to Webcourses. This budget becomes a core component of your Strategic Plan (Week 9, 10 pts), so treat it with that weight.

In PAD 6208, you analyzed an existing nonprofit's financial reports and completed a budget assignment. Now you're building a budget from zero for your own organization.

Step 3: Build Your Budget in a Spreadsheet

Use Google Sheets or Excel. A narrative budget in Word won't cut it — funders expect numbers in a spreadsheet format. If you're in Google Sheets, try Google Gemini in Sheets to help write formulas and create charts — for example, ask it to "create a pie chart from cells B2:B8 with labels from A2:A8."

What Do I Submit?

1. Revenue Projections (Year 1)

Identify your revenue sources and estimate amounts. For a startup nonprofit, be realistic — you won't have a major donor base or a history of grant funding on day one. Look up comparable organizations on GuideStar / Candid (candid.org) — their Form 990s show actual revenue breakdowns by source. Use Candid's Foundation Directory to research foundations that fund organizations like yours, including average grant sizes and application deadlines — access it free through the UCF Library databases (search "Foundation Directory Online" in the library's database list). The Nonprofit Finance Fund (nff.org) also offers free startup-specific budgeting guides and cash flow templates.

AI Tip: Budget Template

Use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini to generate a starting framework:

Create a Year 1 operating budget for a startup nonprofit focused
on community navigation services for immigrant families. Annual
budget: $200,000. Include: 5 staff positions with salary ranges,
program costs, occupancy, technology, insurance, and marketing.
Format as a table with amount and percentage columns.

Use the output as a starting framework, then adjust based on your specific organization and local cost data (check Indeed or Idealist for salary benchmarks in your area).

3. Budget Narrative (Brief)

Write 1 page explaining your budget rationale:

  • Why did you allocate resources this way?
  • What are the biggest financial risks in Year 1?
  • What assumptions are you making?
  • How does this budget support the strategic goals you'll articulate in Week 9?

Website Update

Add a "Transparency" or "Financials" page to your website. Funders and donors increasingly expect financial transparency from nonprofits — even new ones. Getting this on your site now builds credibility from day one.

Your page should include:

  • A summary of your projected Year 1 budget — use Canva to create a clean pie chart or infographic that's more visually engaging than a raw spreadsheet
  • A statement about financial accountability
  • Link to a downloadable budget summary (PDF)
