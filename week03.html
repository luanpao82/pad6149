What Is This Assignment?

This week you build the digital home for your nonprofit. By the end of the week, your organization should have a live, publicly accessible website — even if it's minimal.

This isn't a web design class, and you don't need any coding experience. You'll use a free website builder and AI tools to generate content and visuals. The site starts simple and grows throughout the semester as you complete each deliverable.

Why a website? Because in 2026, a nonprofit without a web presence doesn't exist. Funders check your site before writing a check. Partners check it before returning your call. Your website is your organization's first impression — and for this course, it's also the portfolio that demonstrates your capstone work.

How Your Website and Assignments Work Together

Starting in Week 4, every major assignment follows this pattern:

  1. Write the full document in Word (your graded submission to Webcourses)
  2. Save as PDF and upload it to your website
  3. Write a short, public-facing summary directly on the relevant web page
  4. Add a download link to the full document

This mirrors how real nonprofits operate. Visit any established nonprofit's website — you'll find concise, accessible summaries on their public pages, with full policy documents available as PDF downloads for those who want the details.

You're not doing double work. You're producing one deliverable in two formats: an academic version (Word, for grading) and a professional version (web summary + PDF, for your site). By the end of the semester, your website will look like a real organization's — because it's built the way a real organization would build it.

Choosing a Website Builder

You need a free platform that lets you publish a multi-page site without coding. Here are the options:

Platform Best For Pros Cons
Google Sites (sites.google.com) Fastest setup Free, no ads, integrates with Google Workspace Limited design flexibility
Carrd.co Clean one-page sites Beautiful templates, very simple Free tier is one page only
Wix.com Design flexibility Drag-and-drop, lots of templates Free tier shows Wix branding
WordPress.com Long-term scalability Most powerful, huge template library Steeper learning curve
GitHub Pages Technical users Full control, professional URL Requires basic HTML

Recommendation: If you want the fastest path to a live site, use Google Sites. If you care about aesthetics and want something portfolio-worthy, consider Wix or WordPress.com.

What Your Site Should Include This Week

The goal is a functional foundation — not a finished product. You'll add depth each week.

Required pages/sections:

1. Home Page - Organization name (prominent) - Mission statement - A brief description of what you do and who you serve - A hero image or visual (use Canva AI to generate one)

2. About Us - Expand on your Concept Statement from Week 1 - The problem you're addressing and why it matters - Your approach — what makes this organization's perspective distinctive

3. Placeholder Pages Create the following pages with titles and a brief "Coming soon" note. You'll populate them in later weeks: - Board / Leadership - Our Team - Programs - Get Involved - Donate / Support Us - Advocacy / Take Action

Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Home Page Content

Hero section: "Building Bridges, Strengthening Communities"

Mission: Asian Bridge Orlando provides culturally responsive social services, language access programs, and community navigation support for Asian American and immigrant families in Central Florida.

About: Central Florida's Asian American and immigrant population has grown significantly over the past decade, yet culturally and linguistically appropriate services remain scarce. Many families — particularly those with limited English proficiency — struggle to access healthcare, legal aid, public benefits, and educational resources. Asian Bridge Orlando exists to close that gap, connecting families to the services and systems they need while building community across cultures.

Step-by-Step: Building Your Site with Google Sites

If you're using Google Sites (recommended for speed), here's the workflow:

Step 1: Go to sites.google.com → Click the + button to create a blank site

Step 2: Enter your organization name as the site title

Step 3: Add a hero banner — upload an image or use a stock photo. For free, high-quality stock photos, try Unsplash (unsplash.com) or Pexels (pexels.com) — both are free for commercial use. For a custom image, use Canva: - Go to canva.com → search "website banner" - Use Magic Design or text-to-image with a prompt like: "Community gathering, diverse Asian American families, warm tones, professional nonprofit"

Step 4: Add your mission statement and About Us text below the banner

Step 5: Create additional pages using the Pages panel on the right: - Click + → Add page → Name each one (Board, Team, Programs, etc.) - Add a placeholder line: "This page is under development. Check back as our organization grows."

Step 6: Click Publish → Choose a URL (e.g., sites.google.com/view/asianbridgeorlando)

Step 7 (post-publish): Run your live URL through WAVE (wave.webaim.org) — a free accessibility checker that flags missing alt text, low contrast, and heading structure issues. Fix what it flags. Then add a favicon using Favicon.io (favicon.io) — generate a small icon from your org's initials so the browser tab looks polished. Finally, paste your web copy into the Hemingway Editor (hemingwayapp.com) to check readability — web visitors scan, so aim for clear, concise prose at a Grade 8–10 reading level.

Total time: 30–60 minutes (plus 15 minutes for the polish steps).

Creating Your Visual Identity

Your site needs a basic brand identity. You don't need a graphic designer — AI tools handle this well.

Logo

Creating a Logo with Canva AI

Go to canva.com → Search "logo" → Select a template or use Magic Design:

Create a professional logo for a nonprofit called [Your Org Name].
The organization serves [population] and focuses on [issue].
Style: clean, modern, trustworthy. Colors: [your preferences].

Download as PNG with transparent background.

Brand Colors

Choose 2–3 colors that reflect your organization's tone: - A primary color (dominant — headers, buttons) - A secondary color (accents) - A neutral (background, body text)

Not sure where to start? Coolors.co generates harmonious color palettes — enter one color you like, lock it, and keep generating until you find a combination that fits your organization's tone. For typography, browse Google Fonts (fonts.google.com) to find a professional font pairing — one for headings, one for body text.

Running Example: Asian Bridge Orlando — Brand Identity

  • Primary: Deep teal (#0D7377) — trust, community, professionalism
  • Secondary: Warm gold (#E8A838) — warmth, bridge/connection imagery
  • Neutral: Off-white (#FAFAF7) background, dark charcoal (#1C1917) text
  • Logo concept: A bridge icon connecting two stylized figures, with "Asian Bridge Orlando" in clean sans-serif type

Using AI to Generate Website Copy

You already have your Concept Statement from Week 1. AI can help expand it into website-ready content.

AI Tip: Generate Website Copy

I'm building a website for my nonprofit, Asian Bridge Orlando.
Our mission: [paste your mission statement].
Our target population: [paste from Concept Statement].

Write website copy for:
1. Homepage hero section (2-3 sentences, action-oriented)
2. About Us page (200 words, professional but warm)
3. A "The Problem" section explaining why this org needs to exist (150 words, data-informed)

Tone: professional, community-centered, confident but not aggressive.

Review and revise the output — make sure it sounds like your organization, not like a template.
